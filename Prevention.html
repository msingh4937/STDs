Prevention

STD is an infection transmitted through sexual contact, caused by bacteria, viruses, or parasites.

STDs are common and it is possible to be infected without being aware because many STDs do not display obvious signs or symptoms.

How to Prevent STDs

How To Prevent HIV/AIDS

To help prevent from having HIV/AIDS, get tested and know your partner's HIV status, have less risky sex, use condoms correctly, limit the number of sexual partners, and don't share injection equipments with others.

How To Prevent Syphilis

To prevent from having Syphilis, practice safe sex, use condoms during any type of sexual contact, avoid sharing needles with others, and should get tested as soon as possible.

How To Prevent HPV

To prevent from having HPV, get vaccinated, use latex condoms the right way every time, adopt a healthy lifestyle, consider circumcision, or be in a mutually monogamous relationship.


STD Prevention

If you get an STD you are more likely to get HIV than someone who is STD-free. This is because the same behaviors and circumstances that may put you at risk for getting an STD can also put you at greater risk for getting HIV.

The only way to avoid STDs is to not have vaginal, anal, or oral sex.


STD Facts

Vaccines are safe, effective, and recommended ways to prevent hepatitis B and HPV. Reducing your number of sex partners can decrease your risk for STDs. Being in a long-term mutually monogamous relationship with an uninfected partner is one of the most reliable ways to avoid STDs.

Mutual Monogamy

Mutual monogamy means that you agree to be sexually active with only one person, who has agreed to be sexually active only with you.
